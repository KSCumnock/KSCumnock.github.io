JOB TITLE

While you may be employed initially as a certain Trades Person or Semi-Skilled Person you may be called upon to perform other reasonable tasks to accommodate the needs of the Company.

PAY

Wages will be paid (weekly/monthly), one (week/month) in arrears unless agreed otherwise with the Company. Your (pay/salary) will be paid at the rate agreed individually and reviewed annually in line with Company policy.

HOURS OF WORK

Your normal working hours will be as agreed and displayed on your department rota sheet.

OVERTIME

The nature of the Company's operation is such that the co-operation of all Employees in working overtime is essential. You may therefore be required to work overtime to satisfy our Customers requirements. Management will endeavour to give reasonable notice when overtime is required. All overtime outside normal working hours will be paid at an agreed enhanced rate.

TEA / LUNCH BREAKS

Included in all working hours is a 10 minute morning tea break and a lunch at suitably agreed times as per rota sheet.

HOLIDAY ENTITLEMENT

Employee holidays are earned on a pro-rata basis per month worked. 1/12 of your holiday entitlement is earned per month employed; holidays are calculated on the basis of 2.5 days including statutory days, i.e. total 30 days per year. Holidays run from January to December (e.g. an employee starting in October would be entitled to 3/12 of 30 days, i.e. 7.5 days). In the event of employment termination, holidays taken in excess of entitlement shall be rebated in the final wage reconciliation. Holiday application procedures are described in full in section A3 of the Company booklet.

HOLIDAY PAY

Will be calculated using your average basic pay including guaranteed overtime.

SICK PAY ENTITLEMENT

Will be in line with current legislation unless stated otherwise.

TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT

Notice of termination of employment must be given in writing. Unless otherwise stated, the following notice periods apply (except in the case of dismissal for gross misconduct):

Period of serviceNotice from CompanyNotice from Employee
Up to one monthNilNil
One month but less than 2 yearsOne weekOne week
More than 2 yearsOne week for each complete year of service up to a maximum of 12 weeks4 weeks

DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE

The Disciplinary Procedure is described in full in Section D of the Company Handbook.

APPEAL

Any appeal must be in writing and submitted to the Managing Director no more than 2 working days after the initial warning. There is no appeal procedure above Director level.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Employees are bound not to disclose any Company information both in employment and after termination.

PRIVILEGES

All Company concessions (i.e. paying for vehicles/tools etc.) shall be settled in full before leaving the employment of the Company unless agreed otherwise between both parties.

PENSION

The Company operates a management contributory Pension Scheme. Details of this and of our Stakeholder pension arrangements are available on request.

DEDUCTIONS

The Company reserves the right to deduct any outstanding sums owed by you on termination of employment (including holidays taken in excess of entitlement) from any monies owing to you at the time.

COLLECTIVE AGREEMENTS

Your terms and conditions of employment are not governed by a Collective Agreement.

CHANGES TO TERMS & CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

The Company reserves the right to make variations to your terms and conditions of employment on giving not less than four weeks' written notice.

COMPANY HANDBOOK

The Company Handbook gives details of the terms of employment applying to all employees, including terms for sick pay, discipline and grievance and other important issues. Please read this carefully and ask your Manager if any part is not clear.
